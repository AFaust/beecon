I'm registered. What's next?

We are thrilled that the time for BeeCon has finally arrived!

Though we are saddened by the recent attacks in Brussels, we are proud to be able to show solidarity with our colleagues by hosting BeeCon in their great city.

This page summarizes information you need before arriving at the conference. We sent this information to all attendees via email last week, but message can get into the spam, because there are many links in the text.


Travel and Accommodations

You may encounter travel disruptions during your journey to BeeCon. Specifically, the airport is not yet operating at full capacity. You can get more information here: http://beecon.buzz/travel/#restrictions.

The hotels closest to the conference venue are filling up, so make sure to get yours booked in advance. You are welcome to stay in any hotel, but our list of recommended hotels are here: http://beecon.buzz/travel/#hotels.

Sessions and Activities

During some parts of the conference, there are three concurrent tracks. So you should review the agenda and plan which sessions you want to attend: http://beecon.buzz/agenda/. There are a few special sessions and activities that you should be aware of.

Agenda


Hand-on Workshops: Thursday will feature a series of hand-on workshops running alongside the regular sessions. These workshops take an in-depth look at interesting technologies to add to your Alfresco environment such as GroovyRunner, A.A.A.R. Analytics and Yeoman. If you plan on attending a hands-on workshop, you should bring a laptop.

Agenda


Hack-a-thon: Friday will feature a hack-a-thon running alongside the sessions. The hack-a-thon is a great way to get practical experience with Alfresco by working alongside seasoned developers.

Though the hack-a-thon will run the entire day, you can take a break to attend that session you are excited about. Review projects and join a team here: http://beecon.buzz/special-sessions/#hackathon.

Hack-a-thon


Birds of a Feather: Is there a topic that you wish was on the schedule? Propose it as a Bird of a Feather (BoF) session. We have a limited number of meeting rooms, and will advertise the BoFs so that other interested people can join you. See the list of Bird of a Feather sessions at the registration desk.

Birds of a Feather


Post-Conference Activity: Extend your stay through Saturday, and enjoy an informal tour of medieval city Brugges. We meet at 10h10 on April 30 outside the main entrance of Brussels-Central station. Tickets are about 16 euro per person (round trip).

We will be back in Brussels around 17h50 and 18h00. If you need to be back earlier in Brussels, it is possible. We will never be too far away from the train station. There are two trains per hour. The trip takes between 1h10 to 1h20 minutes.
