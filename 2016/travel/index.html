Brussels travel restrictions


Brussels National Airport of Zaventem is progressively recovering after the attacks of March 22nd. It will not be fully operational by the time of BeeCon, so chances are that already booked flights will be rescheduled to a near-by airport.

  • If you get redirected to another airport in Belgium (Antwerp, Liège, Charleroi), there are always bus-shuttles and trains from those cities to Brussels, at least every hour.
  • If you are travelling from UK, a good option is to take the Eurostar, which traffic has not been affected.
  • If you are coming from (or your flight has been redirected to) Paris or Amsterdam, you can take the Thalys or a regular train. Thalys is faster but more expensive and requires a reservation. There are regular trains every two hours. You don't need a reservation but it takes longer. That website also gives you information about the Thalys, and IZY Thalys, a cheaper fast-train between Paris and Brussels.

If you need more information coming from another city, please contact us.
